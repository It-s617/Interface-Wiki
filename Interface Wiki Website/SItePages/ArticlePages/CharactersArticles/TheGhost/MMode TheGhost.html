Analysis

The Ghosts Deal with Mischief

The Ghost requests that Mischief brings Henryk back to how he used to be, presumably meaning when he was less depressed. She also commands that Mischief informs Henryk of his grate-granddaughters survival.

In exchange for this Mischief will receive all that perish, the meaning of this is not entirely clear but it seems more than likely that this refers to the cerebral electricity in the interface, the interfaces is a form of digital afterlife that “all that perish” will presumably go to.

This would also explain The Ghost’s actions in episode 4 as she shows no resistance to being absorbed to the Greetings Robotics system. This appears odd in the series be could be explained by this being a part of their deal.

Mischief mentions in Episode 24 that he was worried about the consequences of Henryk not waking up this may imply that there are established consequences of breaking the deal that are not made clear in the series. Additionally, it is possible that this would only result in him not getting what The Ghost promised him.

Mischief likely could not have beaten The Mechanical Saccorhytus without being consumed by him and corrupting him, which is odd considering that The Mechanical Saccorhytus seams to be made with the intent of being able to absorb large amounts of cerebral electricity so Mischief should not be able to corrupt him. This leads me to believe that as part of their deal when Henryk and his greet-granddaughter are reunited she will somehow disable whatever was protecting The Mechanical Saccorhytus to allow Mischief to consume the Cerebral Electricity from The Interface.


Shiva Comparison

The Ghost is compared to a Hindu goddess called shiva by Greetings Robotics.

I believe that the origine of this is form the quote, “We knew the world would not be the same. A few people laughed; a few people cried. Most people were silent. I remembered the line from the Hindu scripture, the Bhagavad Gita; Vishnu is trying to persuade the prince that he should do his duty, and to impress him, takes on his multiarmed form and says, ‘Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds.’ I suppose we all thought that, one way or another.”

This quote is highly relevant to the series as it is about the change to the world after the creation of nuclear bombs, which allegorically relates to interfaces which is about the changes to the world after the discovery of cerebral electricity.

From what I have read Shiva is a different form of Vishnu and another god called Rudra. Noticeably all these gods are portrayed as having multiple arms which relates to The Ghost as she has many hands.
