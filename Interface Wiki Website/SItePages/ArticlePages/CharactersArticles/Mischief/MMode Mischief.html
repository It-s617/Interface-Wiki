Description

Physical Appearance

Before his transformation Proto-Mischief looked mostly like a normal human with most of his features covered by clown makeup and his clown attire.

After his transformation Mischief usually appears as a bright pink worm like creature with yellow horn-like things on his forehead, a big smile with yellow teeth and a clown nose.

Mischief is commonly shown to shapeshift meaning he can look like anything, usually he limits himself to being pink, but he has been confirmed to be able to change colours in Episode 11 Revelations and Episode 5 The Son of Man.


Personality

Mischief is very outgoing and friendly, but he is not above being deceptive. Mischief can be somewhat self-interested and can struggle to control his impulses but is not shown to be actively malicious.

Analysis

The Ghosts Deal with Mischief

The Ghost requests that Mischief brings Henryk back to how he used to be, presumably meaning when he was less depressed. She also commands that Mischief informs Henryk of his grate-granddaughters survival.

In exchange for this Mischief will receive all that perish, the meaning of this is not entirely clear but it seems more than likely that this refers to the cerebral electricity in the interface, the interfaces is a form of digital afterlife that “all that perish” will presumably go to.

This would also explain The Ghost’s actions in episode 4 as she shows no resistance to being absorbed to the Greetings Robotics system. This appears odd in the series be could be explained by this being a part of their deal.

Mischief mentions in Episode 24 that he was worried about the consequences of Henryk not waking up this may imply that there are established consequences of breaking the deal that are not made clear in the series. Additionally, it is possible that this would only result in him not getting what The Ghost promised him.

Mischief likely could not have beaten The Mechanical Saccorhytus without being consumed by him and corrupting him, which is odd considering that The Mechanical Saccorhytus seams to be made with the intent of being able to absorb large amounts of cerebral electricity so Mischief should not be able to corrupt him. This leads me to believe that as part of their deal when Henryk and his greet-granddaughter are reunited she will somehow disable whatever was protecting The Mechanical Saccorhytus to allow Mischief to consume the Cerebral Electricity from The Interface.



Was Mischief Lying to Henryk

When the details of Mischief deal with The Ghost where reviled it appears that he was lying to Henryk. However, I do not agree with this I will explain why bellow.

The main reasons people think he is lying are his actions before Episode 11, he doesn’t tell Henryk about the deal with The Ghost and makes it seem like he has just interested in Henryk, during Episode 5 he makes it seem like he doesn’t know what Henryk wants when he should know the sources of Henryk trauma. However, Mischief never lies about knowing where Henryk’s Greet-Granddaughter, Henryk never asks, and Mischief tells him as soon as he feels that he understands Henryk. Additional in episode 8 we see that Mischief was unaware of Henryk’s age and it seams like he has generally pore knowledge on Henryk beyond what happened during the crash that killed most of Henryk’s family.

To me this makes it seem a lot more like Mischief was trying to learn more about Henryk and what drives him before reviling his greet granddaughter survived. This makes sense considering that one of the key terms of Mischifes deal with The Ghost seams to be that he will make Henryk happy again, so it would be important to get to know him before dropping a massive piece of information like that, which may have been destressing to Henryk.

So in short well Mischief was clearly being dishonest I don’t think it is fair to say that he was lying or being malicious before episode 11.

Sunflowers Are Mischief, Mischief Is a Dragon, And Dragons Are Death

This episode has what I believe to be a three-layered metaphor about Death, Dragons, Mischief, And Sunflowers I will attempt to explain here.


Layer one, death is a dragon

When The Ringmaster is giving the fine part of his monolog he clearly corelates the dragon with death, The Ringmaster claims that he saw death in his dream but then the story’s about a dragon and if you listening to the monologue its very clear that the dragon is meant to be a metaphor for death. At an earlier point in the monologue The Ringmaster claims that people may have seen death at the bottom of a bottle, this is another example of a similar metaphor to help prove my point.

Layer two, Mischief is a dragon

Mischief being a dragon is easier to explain and prove, in the end of the episode we see The Ringmaster introduces the dragon he apparently tamed before Proto-Mischief is shown. I believe that only would prove the connection but there is more proof at one point in the monologue The Ringmaster says “That’s when I saw it! It twisted and twirled towards me. I saw Its wicked smile, I heard its wicked laugh!” this is clearly talking about Mischief as he is known to travel in a similar manner and is known to have a sinister smile.

Additionally, I believe that Mischief as a dragon still represents death, the part of the monologue that most directly talks about death is as the part that most directly talks about death, the line of the monolog being the line about the dragon twisting and twirling. The way The Ringmaster talks about the dragon makes it seem like his talking about death as a separate entity to the dragon, but the sentence would only really makes sense if he is talking about both. It is important to remember that The Ringmaster is also clearly talking about Mischief, so it only makes sense that Mischief represents both the dragon and death.


Layer three, Mischief is a sunflower

Before getting into this episode, I feel it is important to remind you of when Mischief transformed into a sunflower in episode five and says, “I’m a beautiful sunflower”, it doesn’t really get clearer than that but there is more proof of the metaphor in episode fourteen.

In the episode we see a scene where the shot is constantly changing between Proto-Mischief and a sunflower that may imply that the two are being corelated. We also see another scene where we see Proto-Mischief walking down a corridor with his reflection being replaced by a sunflower. Well ever of these two examples could mean nothing on their own it, but taking both metaphors together with the scene from episode five it is a much clearer corelation between sunflowers and Mischief.

This leaves the question of if sunflowers are also meant to corelate to dragons and death my answer yes-ish.

I believe the dragon analogy purely apples to Mischief in this one scene. Mischief clearly isn’t a dragon, I think the dragon is just a metaphor for death used in the monologue as a way to avoid directly calling Mischief death, in simple terms I believe that if Mischief is not a dragon, is death and Sunflowers are Mischief then sunflowers must represent death and Mischief but not dragons.
