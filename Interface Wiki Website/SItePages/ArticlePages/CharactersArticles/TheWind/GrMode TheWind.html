Personal Timeline

This segment will be going over all the timeline of The Wind in order. I will do this as extensively as possible so some speculation will be included but this will be shown as speculation to the best of my ability.

Below is an explanation for how the timeline works.


All events will be in chronological order if possible.


If multiple events happen in the same year that will be numbered in the order they occur, explanations below for clarity.

Events that happen within an episode not a flashback will have the episode number after the year.

if the order is unclear or there are no other events then there will be no number to signify what the order they occurred in.

in cases where only the order of some events are clear, the events that have no clear order will share a number.


The date is as specific as it is written, explanations below for clarity.

If a year is stated with no additional information that is the exact year it happened.

If a year is said to be before a year it is that year or any point before.

If a year is said to be after a year it is that year or any point after.

If a year is said to be around a year it is near that year but may be a few years before or after.

The Wind’s Vision

In this segment I will be analysis The Winds monologue from Episode 15 The Winds and explain what I believe it means.

I think that the directions correlate to time in this monologue with north representing the present and futter, east representing the future, south representing the past and present, and lastly west represent the past. Most of the following Analysis still works regardless of this being true but this dose helps the theory.

For north he says “In this land of rot and erosion” as Henryk’s faces is shown.

This may refer to all of Canada or moder times in general regardless of it implies the current world and land is in a state of decay.

for east he says “You stand alone for” the scene showing dragonflies resembling Henryk and Mischief.

This could be implied to refer to Henryk in the future, saying he will be alone. This makes some sense as in episode 24 Mischief is believed to sacrifice himself to Henryk however Henryk still has his granddaughter so he cannot be said to be truly alone.

for south he says “static, stoic, frozen” showing a scene with Henryk by a plane with two men in military uniforms, then a picture with Henryk and his with and as final image of them holding hands before the female hand disappears leaving only the ring.

This is clearly referring to Henryk’s incorrigibility and how the world has moved around him. We see images from Henryk’s past moving forward which falls in line with this line being about Henryk’s incurability and the direction correlating to time.

for west he says “I scan the horizon... not heavens skies nor cavers below… I see your home a stat of abandon there is no judgment only dangers aglow the shot changes to focuses on Henryk for most of this scene.

This Is the segment I am the least clear on but if the direction correlating to time theory is accurate then this segment would seemingly be referring to the destruction of Henryk’s family. This is further supported by Henryk’s daughter comments on Henryk’s immortality being played during this scene.

All together I believe that this monologue is talking about the Henryk’s shame in the death of his family, well enviably everybody in Henryk’s family would die, we see in episode twenty that Henryk has directly responsible for the death of two members of his family and the loss of Henryk’s Great-Granddaughter.
