Description

Physical Appearance

Henryk appears as a middle-aged man with red hair and moustache, nearly always seen in a blue suit and bowler hat.


Personality

Henryk appears to be depressed, quiet, and contemplative, never speaking in the Interface series. It is clear from his actions that more than everything he cares for his family and feels intense guilt for accidently killing his grandchild and their partner.

Analysis

Henryk Is Polish

Henryk is likely polish or from a polish family. His family are known to cock perogies and his name is polish.

Given the importance of World War 2 in the series this could imply that Henryk was one of the many refugees created by the Nazi invasion of Poland. This is not conferred or disproven by the series so far.



How Did Henryk Survive His Suicide Attempted

We see Henryk in the road before the crash and then it cuts to white, there is not enough spaces or time available for the car to turn into the river like we see it did.

This implies a part of his powers may be protecting him from harm in whatever way is possible. In this case I believe that Henryk realised a large amount of Cerebral Electricity and blow the car of the road, this is also supported by the fact we see a lot of Cerebral Electricity on the bridge after the crash. In episode 13 we see Henryk perfectly land after a fall which could be a much smaller use of this power.



Henryks Death Is Very Complicated

During one scene from episode 23 we see Henryk getting blasted by a nuke casing him to die before getting covered with Cerebral Electricity and reviving.

This would seemingly imply that Henryk died to a nuclear blast, but we also know that Henryk was transformed when the sky’s where still blue, meaning before 1943.

This is odd as the first nuke was detonated in 1945, meaning that unless the events of when the skies where still blue are before Henryk’s transformation Henryk dying to a nuke seemingly cannot be true.

The most likely conclusion is that this is a small continuity error and that UMAMI did not realize that the first bomb test was 1945 but I think this could also imply that in the interfaces universe the early nuclear bomb testes where more successful than in our universe.

This could be explained by the presents of cerebral electricity in Interface if cerebral electricity or water weas used to power The Philadelphia Experiment was used instead of uranium this could explain how the development of nuclear blasts was quicker in Interface.

Amusing the bomb was seemingly powered by cerebral electricity would also explain how Henryk became a Cerebral Electricity Monster before The Philadelphia Experiment, as well as why the nuclear blast was likely bigger than the military expected.

Henryk transformation into a cerebral electricity monster prior to the Philadelphia experiment is not a contradiction as ghost stories and superstitions are explained to have always been a result of cerebral electricity but where only provable after the experiment acquired.

I believe that the nuclear blast was larger than the military expected because of Henryk’s death the military wouldn’t tell Henryk to go somewhere they know he would die the only explanation is that they didn’t think the blast would be as big as it was. This may add to the evidence that the material used for bomb wasn’t uranium but something related to cerebral electricity.

A crazier theory for Henryk somehow dying to a nuke years before they were made is that Henryk may have time travelled after his death, he appears out of nowhere in episode 16 and this is the simplest explanation for this seaming continuity error additionally Cerebral electricity is shown to be able to effect time, as we see The Wind can freeze time, all that being said this is pretty blatantly not intended.



Henryk Represents The Son of Man

Henryk is clearly meant to represent The Son of Man in the show.

I believe there is a correlation between the apparent boringness of the original Son of Man with Henryk also seeming boring at first glance. In both cases it is not the appearance of the man that is meant to be interesting but the hidden details that are only hinted at in the original work. This could be supported by the chose to make Henryk a silent protagonist which makes him much more mysterious.

In short Henryk being represented by The Son of Man is a metaphor representing that Henryk is a lot more important and interesting than he first appears.
