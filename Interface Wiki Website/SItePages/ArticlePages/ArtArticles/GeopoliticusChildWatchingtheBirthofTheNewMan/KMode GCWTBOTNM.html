Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man
Title: Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man
Made by: Salvador Dali
Year Made: 1943
Type: Painting
Description

The painting focuses on an egg hatching in the centre.

the egg is clearly representing the world with countries being shown in yoke.

A man is seen hatching from the egg as other men look and point at the egg.

In the fair background other children are seen playing.

The is a cloth surrounding the egg, likely representing the placenta.

Original Meaning of The Work

The traditional interpretation of the painting is usually that the Geopolitical child represents the world as it was, and The New Man represents the future and the next generation.

Note this painting was made in 1943 so the next generation would be arguable in the past or present, however the painting could be interpreted as applying to the future depending on how it is used.

Analysis of Uses in The Show

Episode 6 Bear Witness

This is compered to both Mischief and Henryk.

For Mischief it is clearly implied that he is The New Man the painting is actually altered to have The New Man be pink like Mischief, Mischief also compares himself to the painting and how when he was reborn, he had changed the world. This encourages the metaphor that Mischief and Cerebral Electricity represent progress and the next generation.

For Henryk it is less clear, but my interpretation is that Henryk represents The Geopolitical Child watching the birth of The New Man. Henryks immortality has lead him to watch the world moving on and transforming around him but not being able to fully change to go along with the times, this is also supported by Henryk’s career being a photographer somebody who’s job is to watch the world and record it.
