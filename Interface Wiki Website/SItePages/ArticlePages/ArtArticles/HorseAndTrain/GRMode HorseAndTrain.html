Horse And Train
Title: Horse And Train
Made by: David Alexander Colville
Year Made: 1954
Type: Painting
Description

The focus of the painting is the dark horses galloping on the train tracks.

In the distance you can see a train approaching the horse.

The painting seams set in a grazing filed with a track running through it.

Original Meaning of The Work

This painting is inspired by a poem by Roy Campbell “I scorn the goose-step of their massed attack And fight with my guitar slung on my back, against a regiment I oppose a brain and a dark horse against an armoured train.”.

The main feature of this painting is the juxtaposition of the horse and the armoured train with the train clearly outmatching the horses in every way, representing the future and the horse representing the past.

Analysis of Uses in The Show

Episode 16 When Skies Were Still Blue

The meaning for this episode seams to be contrasting the horses and Henryk. Henryk is the man of the past and the horse unable to match the new man, as represented by Mischief who likely represents the train.


Episode 23 An Unconscious Conscience

Well, the original use of the painting was to represent Henryk as the man of the past being passed by modern technology, in this case It is obvious that the horse represents Mischief and progress.

The old train representing the past Mr Greetings serves and the horses representing the new world created by Cerebral Electricity.
