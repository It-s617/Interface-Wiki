Two Comedians
Title: Two Comedians
Made by: Edward Hopper
Year Made: 1966
Type: Painting
Description

The painting depicts two comedians on a lavish stage taking a bow.

The comedians are dressed in white and are meant to represent the painter and his wife.

Original Meaning of The Work

The original artwork was painted as a goodbye by Edward Hopper in 1965 as his final painting. This was noticeable the year before his death.

The painting is actually him and his wife not just regular comedians, this could be to insinuate that they were humorous people who were aiming to make people happy like comedians do.

Analysis of Uses in The Show

Episode 22 Ascend to Solitude

In the show it this is references as Mischief say, “Two comedians take a bow, 54 years too soon." and a scene of Proto-Mischief and The Foolish Girl is shown before The Ringmaster is shown looking upset.

This was the year that Mischief presumably stopped being a comedian and an acter, this would be because of the fire that burned down Dreamland, acquiring in 1911.

When he says 54 years to soon, he is referring to the painting Two Comedians which was painted in 1965,
