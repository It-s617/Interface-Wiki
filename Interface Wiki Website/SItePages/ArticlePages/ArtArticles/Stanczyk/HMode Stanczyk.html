Stanczyk
Title: Stanczyk
Made by: Jan Alojzy Matejko
Year Made: 1862
Type: Painting
Description

This painting depicts a depressed looking Jester sitting in a chair after having read the letter on the table.

There is a party that is being held on the right through the door.

Original Meaning of The Work

The most common interpretation of the original painting is the jester is morning the loss of a city that had just been lost to their enemy, this loss possible leading to the doom of the nation. It is important to notice that in the background there is a party clear being held showing that ironically the only person taking the loss of the city seriously is the juster.

The full title of the painting is Stańczyk during a ball at the court of Queen Bona in the face of the loss of Smolensk.

Analysis of Uses in The Show

Episode 14 Dreamland

I believe that there is a significant chance that this painting is just used for the inspiration of the outfit and pose.

That being said I do have one interpretation of the painting based on the original meaning of the painting; Mischief may be morning the loss of his humanity. The original painting clearly shows the clown distraught at the loss of the city to their enemy which raise the question what would Mischief morning? The easiest answer is that he is morning the loss of his humanity, this episode is about Mischief ether dreaming or remembering his time as a human, so it makes sense that if he is sad about this, he may be mentally correlating himself with the painting.

This would not be the only time that we see a painting used to convey meaning without having to say something aloud so it would not be odd for this to be how the painting is used to show how Mischief feels.
