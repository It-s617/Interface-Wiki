Description

This episode focuses on Henryk and Mischief exploring the Montreal Museum of Fine Art and discussing the painting called The Son of Man.

This is the first episode that takes place in the Montreal Museum of Fine Art and the first episode that focuses heavily on artwork for the meaning of the episode.

Summary

Opening

The episode opens with Henryk wearing Mischief in his suit form as they enter the Montreal Museum of Fine Art, they walk up the stairs before find a long hallway filled with Cerebral Electricity.

Henryk and Mischief are then seen to appear in the room with the painting called The Son of Man.

Mischief’s discussion with Henryk

Mischief changes into his primary form and comments on how he has forgotten what it is like to be human and how restrictive he finds the human body. Mischief also comments on that when he sees through Henryk’s eyes the world would look the same too him but that he cannot understand what desires are driving Henryk. Mischief stats that he knows when something is blocking Henryk’s way as the picture of The Son of Man is shown but with Henryk as the person in the painting.

Cerebral Electricity is seen to rise from the river and is consumed by Mischief. The Ghost is then seen to use their hand power to raise Henryk’s great-granddaughter from the wreckage before the memory ends and Henryk’s great-granddaughter is shown to wake up.

Ending

Thay are then interrupted by The Museum Boy and his mother causing Mischief to transform into a sunflower wrapped around a sculpture called The Eye. Mischief talks to the boy telling him he is a beautiful sunflower clearly scaring him before the episode fades to black and ending.

Artworks

The Son of Man

The son of man is the focus of this episode and is likely being used as a metaphor for Mischief not being able to understand what Henryk wants and Henryk not being able to understand modern the world they are in as he is stuck in the past rejecting the world as it is especially after the trauma of losing his family.

The Son of Man can literally mean what comes after man or the next generation, this supports the metaphor that apples may represent the future and the importance of the next generation which will appear more in future episodes.

I believe that this is supported by the quote in the description ("Everything we see hides another thing, we always want to see what is hidden by what we see. There is an interest in that which is hidden and which the visible does not show us. This interest can take the form of a quite intense feeling, a sort of conflict, one might say, between the visible that is hidden and the visible that is present.") and what is shown in this episode as well as other episodes mentioned on the page for The Son of Man

The Eye

There is no description for the statue given in the description other than that the statue is made of bronze, additionally the statue is not focused on in the episode over than when Mischief is wrapped around it, It is for these reasons I believe that this statue was only meant to be set dressing for this episode.
