The Star of All Flames
Title: The Star of All Flames
Released (DMY): 8/4/24
Video Type: Short

Summary

Two orbs are seen to collide.

A pulsing black and white spiral is shown.

A shot with the full poem a sunflower and a sun icon is shown.

A naked man is seen floating in a void.

“Warmth that nourishes,”

a close up of a pink man closing his eyes is shown.

“Yet when turning to face those I admire haste splits my lips,”

We return to the shot with the full poem a sunflower and a un icon is shown.

we return to the shot of the pulsing black and white lights.

“Poison grows, cursed, with vile speech.”

the orbs separate.

Complete Poem

The Star of All Flames, its luminance flourishes, unto my mortal frame, warmth that nourishes

yet when turning to face, those I admire, haste splits my lips, a tongue of desire

it stems from below, not the skies I reach, poison grows, cursed, with vile speech.

Notes

I believe that this video is meant to be in the Interface canon as we frequently see what appears to be cerebral electricity in the video as well as sunflower imagery which are both important to Interface.

The most common interpretation of this poem is that it is making fun of people looking into the sun during the eclipse and hurting themselves.
