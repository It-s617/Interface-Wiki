Summary

Opening

The episode opens on a shot of the house before change to a shot inside the house with a girl playing the piano as Henryk approaches.

The Life of a Tree

The scene changes to a tree paining down this is shown to be Henryk’s wedding. The tree is again shown this time slightly bigger with a girl on the swings in front of it. The scene changes again to the same tree much thicker with Henryk taking a picture of a woman holding a baby surrounded by other people.

Ending

The episode ends with a finale shot of Henryk by the piano looking out The Window with no girl.

Analysis

This episode is seemingly showing the life of Henryk’s daughter also known as The Ghost. In the first scene the lady on the piano is most likely Henryk’s wife the next scene is Henryk marrying his wife before a scene with a young girl on a set a of swings is shown who is likely his daughter and the final scene depicts a women probably Henryk’s daughter holding a child presumable Henryk’s great-granddaughter however this could be his granddaughter daughter holding his granddaughter

This episode also make reference to an old proverb about how you should plant a tree build a house and raise a child. Henryk is shown to do all these things and in a latter episode it is shown that all of these things have since died or bean destroyed implying that Henryk has lived a full life and live long enough to see all of what he has bult be destroyed.

Notes

One of the members of Henryk’s family bears a striking resemblance to KAMI but given the fate of the family. However, in episode elven we learn that this is not possible for any of these character’s to be related to KAMI as their cerebral electricity would have been devoured by Mischief.
