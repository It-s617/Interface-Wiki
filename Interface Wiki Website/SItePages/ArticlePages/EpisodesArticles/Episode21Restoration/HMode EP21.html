Description

This episode focuses on Mr Greetings having an interview, and his memories of The Philadelphia Experiment, and the changes made to the world in its wake. We also get to see The Museum Boy growing a beautiful sunflower.

Summary

Opening

The episode opens on a shot of a burned down area likely Dreamland before focusing on a shot of a charred skull. The scene then suddenly cuts to an interview with Mr Greetings on a show called The Stream.

Mr Greetings Interview and His Memories of The Philadelphia Experiment

The Interviewer introduces Mr Greetings by talking about The Philadelphia Experiment, she claims that Mr Greetings was present for the test as a new recruit to the US military. As she discusses this a newspaper is shown from the immediate aftermath of The Philadelphia Experiment it makes it clear that the public was immediately aware of the paradigm shift as well as confirming The Philadelphia Experiment took Places on the USS Eldridge.

We see a picture of a young Mr Greetings followed by a Photo of an old Mr Greeting before the scene changes to show the Greetings Robotics title card, The Interviewer confirms that Mr Greetings is the founder and CEO of Greetings Robotics, she also confers that the purpose of Greetings Robotics is to restore the world to the way it was before 1943.

A scene of The Museum Boy planting some sunflowers, before The Interviewer begins to discuss the people who were born after 1943 and have only experienced the world as it is. Mr Greetings begins to explain the difficulty of explaining the differences between the world before and after The Philadelphia Experiment to people who did not see the way the world was before. He claims that The Philadelphia Experiment, made much more things visible to people. Mr Greetings claims to believe that something like The Philadelphia Experiment was inevitable was unavoidable and comperes’ Cerebral Electricity to force like nuclear power, the ozone, and climate change.

During Mr Greetings monologue we see a few different shots, the first is a shot of pre–Philadelphia Experiment New York Before a ship materialise and releases a pulse wave of Cerebral Electricity changing the colours of everything it touches.

Still during Mr Greetings monologue the scene changes to show The Museum Boy watching a sunflower grow surprisingly fast eventually seeing it bloomed.

As Mr Greetings finishes his monologue, we see the scene change back to know your during The Philadelphia Experiment, The Statue of Liberty is shown as it suddenly changes from blue to pink, we see a Man floating in front of it completely made of Cerebral Electricity.

We see another wide shot of New York now entirely consumed by the Cerebral Electricity changes, followed by another shot of the floating man by The Statue of Liberty.

The scene changes back to the USS Eldridge as we see a man fuss to the floor of one of the decks. We then see a shot of another man covered in Cerebral Electricity floating slightly above the USS Eldridge before he transforms into Mr Greetings and lands on the Deck of the ship unharmed, we see as he watches the body of a man fused to the hull fall apart seemingly disintegrating into Cerebral Electricity.

We seer planes flying in the orange skies of a post change new York as we see the man floating by The Statue of Liberty again this time much higher, he notices the planes as they begin to open fire, the scene changes to some shots of KAMI before cutting back to a shot of the floating man clearly afraid, we then see another shot of KAMI and the Cerebral Electricity pules wave covering the world.

Finaly, we see the floating man in a pose reminiscent of Satan Descends Upon Earth, as he to falls likely as due to being shot. Mr Greetings is seen to watch him fall from the ship.

Mr Greetings is asked another question by The Interviewer, she asks what he would say to those who are afraid of what’s to come and he replies that the only people who are afraid are the ones born into the post-change world, comparing them to people born into a shadow world and that they will not have a chose as the majority are onboard.

During the final question we see The Museum Boy watching the interview with the sunflower in the background, as Mr Greetings begins to speak we see the flower fall before a close up on the boy and a wide shot of the building he lives in with the sunflower on the pavement outside shattered as Greetings Robotics trucks go by.

Ending

We see Mr Greetings on board the USS Eldridge before we see Mischief rise from the water in a much larger form, Mischief says “Boo.” Before rushing away. This confirms the floating man is Mischief mid-transformation. The episode then cuts to black ending the episode.

Analysis

Newspaper breakdown

The newspaper shown in the beginning of the episode has some easily missed information in it I will transcribe here.

The title says, “WHAT HAVE WE DONE.”.

This is one of the pieces of evidence shown here that implies that the public was immediately aware and horrified by the changes made to the world after The Philadelphia Experiment.

The subtitles that we can read are.



“US SAILORS FUSED TO USS ELDRIDGE.”

This shows that the public are aware of the details of what happened during The Philadelphia Experiment.



“NAVY SILENT ON MILITARY EXPERI-MENT GONE WRONG.”

This shows that the military did not know what happened at first and took time to comment.



“UNEXPLAINED GHOST SIGHTINGS SKYROCKET.”

This shows that there where a verity of Cerebral Electricity Monsters immediately presents after the transformation and that they may have existed before but without people being able to see them.



Sunflowers

We see The Museum Boy growing a sunflower before seeing it fall out The Window well, he is watching the interview with Mr Greetings. I believe that the metaphor’s specified in dreamland apply here and gives the scene a deeper meaning, which I will explained here.

The metaphor in Episode 14 Dreamland was that Mischief represents death and sunflowers represent both Mischief and death. Mischief has also been shown to represent the future and the next generation as shown in “bear wittiness” where he is compered to The New Man, who is literally the generation following the year 1943.

The first scene shows The Museum Boy being interested in growing the sunflower, I believe that this is meant to show that the boy is interested in the future and the progress of the world as said above this is what Mischief represents and sunflowers are represents Mischief.

The second scene shows The Museum Boy watching the Mr Greetings interview as the sunflower falls out of The Window. I believe that this is meant to show that the boy losses interest in the future because of what Mr Greetings is saying in the interview, this is lily what Mr Greetings intends due to his sole goal being to take the world back to the way it was before 1943.

Additionally, I believe that this scene could also be about death, in dreamland this is implied to be something that Mischief represents. So, the scene where he grows the sunflower would represent him expecting death in his life and the scene where the sunflower falls would represent him rejecting death. This makes some sense as The Interface is shown to be a form of digital afterlife meaning that Mr Greetings has essentially ended death in the world.
