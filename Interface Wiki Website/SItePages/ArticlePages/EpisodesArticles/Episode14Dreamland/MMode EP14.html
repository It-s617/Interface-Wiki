Analysis

Dreamland or Foolish House

There is some confusion on whether the venue they are at is called Dreamland or Foolish House, the answer is actual both. In the episode the building clearly has a big sign out front saying Foolish House which would imply it is the name of the business, but The Ringmaster says they are at Dreamland as well as the episode being called that, so this is naturally confusing.

Thankfully, there is a simple explanation Dreamland is the name of an amusement park that Foolish House is located in. Dreamland is actual a real-life theme park that was located on Cony Island, New York until it burned down in 1911. This is directly confirmed by Umami in the description of the episode.



Sunflowers Are Mischief, Mischief Is a Dragon, And Dragons Are Death

This episode has what I believe to be a three-layered metaphor about Death, Dragons, Mischief, And Sunflowers I will attempt to explain here.

Layer one, death is a dragon

When The Ringmaster is giving the fine part of his monolog he clearly corelates the dragon with death, The Ringmaster claims that he saw death in his dream but then the story’s about a dragon and if you listening to the monologue its very clear that the dragon is meant to be a metaphor for death. At an earlier point in the monologue The Ringmaster claims that people may have seen death at the bottom of a bottle, this is another example of a similar metaphor to help prove my point.

Layer two, Mischief is a dragon

Mischief being a dragon is easier to explain and prove, in the end of the episode we see The Ringmaster introduces the dragon he apparently tamed before Proto-Mischief is shown. I believe that only would prove the connection but there is more proof at one point in the monologue The Ringmaster says “That’s when I saw it! It twisted and twirled towards me. I saw Its wicked smile, I heard its wicked laugh!” this is clearly talking about Mischief as he is known to travel in a similar manner and is known to have a sinister smile.

Additionally, I believe that Mischief as a dragon still represents death, the part of the monologue that most directly talks about death is as the part that most directly talks about death, the line of the monolog being the line about the dragon twisting and twirling. The way The Ringmaster talks about the dragon makes it seem like his talking about death as a separate entity to the dragon, but the sentence would only really makes sense if he is talking about both. It is important to remember that The Ringmaster is also clearly talking about Mischief, so it only makes sense that Mischief represents both the dragon and death.

Layer three, Mischief is a sunflower

Before getting into this episode, I feel it is important to remind you of when Mischief transformed into a sunflower in episode five and says, “I’m a beautiful sunflower”, it doesn’t really get clearer than that but there is more proof of the metaphor in episode fourteen.

In the episode we see a scene where the shot is constantly changing between Proto-Mischief and a sunflower that may imply that the two are being corelated. We also see another scene where we see Proto-Mischief walking down a corridor with his reflection being replaced by a sunflower. Well ever of these two examples could mean nothing on their own it, but taking both metaphors together with the scene from episode five it is a much clearer corelation between sunflowers and Mischief.

This leaves the question of if sunflowers are also meant to corelate to dragons and death my answer yes-ish.

I believe the dragon analogy purely apples to Mischief in this one scene. Mischief clearly isn’t a dragon, I think the dragon is just a metaphor for death used in the monologue as a way to avoid directly calling Mischief death, in simple terms I believe that if Mischief is not a dragon, is death and Sunflowers are Mischief then sunflowers must represent death and Mischief but not dragons.



Proto-Mischief’s Name

When Proto-Mischief is announced on stage his name is said to be Percy, but Proto-Mischief is confused by this clamming to have changed that.

To explain this, I think it helps to understand that this episode is implied to be a dream or heavily distorted memory of Mischief post transformation in a cerebral electricity monster, so the events are not literal. Percy was likely the stage name of Proto-Mischief but Mischief remembering or dreaming is confused by this as he has changed his name to Mischief.

Additionally, it is possible that Proto-Mischief did actually just change his stage name at some point but for the reasons said above I believe that this is meant to apply to Mischief dreaming of his time as a human.
