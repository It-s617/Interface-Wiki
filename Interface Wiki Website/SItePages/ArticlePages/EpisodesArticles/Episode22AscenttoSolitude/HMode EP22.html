Summary

Opening

The episode opens on a shot of the lighthouse pulsing with Cerebral Electricity, Henryk stairs at it for a while before Mischief comments on the lighthouse making sound, the lighthouse door is seen opening before Mischief asks if the lighthouse is beckoning them.

Henryk is seen to enter The Lighthouse and Mischief mentions that Henryk will have to take the stairs and is seen to change into his bird from to fly up as flapping is “good for the cardio”.

Mischifes Monologue on The Past

Mischief begins to monologue about the past as Henryk is seen climbing up the Lighthouse and Mischief is seen flapping. I will quote Mischief directly for clarity.

Mischief says "The year was 1911. Two comedians take a bow, 54 years too soon." and a scene of Proto-Mischief and The Foolish Girl is shown before The Ringmaster is shown looking upset.

Mischief says "1943. Employers shot me down. I've already told you that one." And a scene is shown of Henryk climbing the stairs is shown before a shot of Mischief looking into camera is shown.

Mischief says "1946. The demon core struck twice. Will it strike once more?" Henryk is seen to reach the top of the lighthouse and see a column of Cerebral Electricity.

Mischief says "1954. The apple was a ruse." A shot of an apple falling before shattering into Cerebral Electricity is shown.

Mischief says "1961. My soul, where are you? I will wander with you and ascend to my solitude." Henryk is shown to touch the column before faces appear in the Cerebral Electricity. Mischief then asks When it was for Henryk as Henryk steres into the far distances.

Ending

We see The Mechanical Saccorhytus appears in the far ground as the shot focuses in on it and it begins to transform, releasing a massive amount of Cerebral Electricity. Hands spread out from the column in the light houses as they around Henryk and The Ghost is heard to say “dad”. The scene is consumed by Cerebral Electricity before the episode ends.

Analysis

Mischifes monologue

Mischief monologue makes references to several events and dates without elaborating much so I will go over some interpretations of each line here.



Mischief says "The year was 1911. Two comedians take a bow, 54 years too soon." and a scene of Proto-Mischief and The Foolish Girl is shown before The Ringmaster is shown looking upset.

This was the year that Mischief presumably stopped being a comedian and an acter, this would be because of the fire that burned down dreamland, acquiring in 1911.

It is not clear what the cause of the fire was in the show, in real life we know it was an accident caused by pore fire safety and regulations not directly blameable on anybody. However, in the show Proto-Mischief and The Ringmaster are implied to have had something to do with it, possibly over drama with The Foolish Girl. Too be clear this is only ever implied by the show and never confirmed or directly shown.

When he says 54 years to soon, he is referring to the painting Two Comedians which was painted in 1965, by Edward Hopper, as his final painting and goodbye.



Mischief says "1943. Employers shot me down. I've already told you that one." And a scene is shown of Henryk climbing the stairs is shown before a shot of Mischief looking into camera is shown.

This is referring to The Philadelphia Experiment when Mischief transformed into a Cerebral Electricity Monster before being shot down by the military.

When he says I’ve already told you that one it’s because he has in episode one.



Mischief says "1946. The demon core struck twice. Will it strike once more?" Henryk is seen to reach the top of the lighthouse and see a column of Cerebral Electricity.

This refers to an event in 1946 where there were two accidents caused by an unexploded nuclear core called “The Demon Core” which emitted a large amount of radiation killing two people.

It is not clear what the third strike is meant to represents but it is clear that it’s meant to represent a possible coming disaster.

One possible meaning of this could be KAMI’s planed attack on Henryk and Mischief, the first two strikes of the demon core would be her absorbing The Ghost and The Chef, with the third possible strike being her planed attack on the duo.



Mischief says "1954. The apple was a ruse." A shot of an apple falling before shattering into Cerebral Electricity is shown.

This refers to the death of Alan Turing in 1954 he was a prominent computer scientists and war hero. Some believe that his death has caused by him having been assassinated by a poisoned apple or that he killed himself with the same apple, though this is widely believed to be false as it doesn’t hold up to scrutiny.

The most likely answers to his death in the real world was an accident he was experimenting with cyanide and if he consumed even a little by accident he would have died. It is worth noting that next to no research was done on the body of Alan Turing was done so it is hard to confirm for sure what killed him, and it is entirely possible his death was nothing to do with cyanide or the apple.

It is unclear what this is meant to mean in the series, but it likely has something to do with the painting called The Son of Man meaning the apple hiding The Son of Man is a lie.

The son of man in the show is likely meant to refer to two things Mischief not understanding Henryk’s desires and the importance of the next generation.

This line makes more scenes when applied to the first meaning we know that Mischief always intended to bring Henryk to Henryk’s great-granddaughter as this was a part of his deal with The Ghost, so the ruse was presumably referring to Mischief tricking Henryk as he tried to get to know him better.

The importance of the next generation doesn’t apply as well in this scene but could refer to the importance of progress for all generations not just the news generation.



Mischief says "1961. My soul, where are you? I will wander with you and ascend to my solitude." Henryk is shown to touch the column before faces appear in the Cerebral Electricity.

This references the red book in 1961 by Carl Jung the book heavily focuses on the importance of self-realisation. Which seems to be an important concept to Mischief.
