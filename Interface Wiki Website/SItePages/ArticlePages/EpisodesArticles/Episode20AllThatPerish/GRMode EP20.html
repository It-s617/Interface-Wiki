Synopsis: We see what it was that put Henryk over the edge and Mischief 's true motivations become a bit more clear. Is he as harmless as he seems? Is Greetings Robotics doing the world a favour?

Summary

Opening

The episode opens on as scene of the river Henryk’s family will crash in as The Ghosty begins her monologue.

The Ghost’s Monologue

The Ghost says that with every year that passes Henryk becomes more quiet. the scene changes to be a shot of Henryk in a room with his daughter and another with Henryk pushing her on a swing likely when she was much younger, during which she claims to have grown older than her father. The Ghost claims that Henryk only looks the same but is changing with time likely getting sadder.

We see several shots of crows and a shot of Henryk’s daughter clearly incredibly old. The Lighthouses is shown before a final shot of Henryk’s daughter is shown with cloth covering her faces, her cerebral electricity is released before collecting above the house and forming a hand. This confirms that Henryks daughter is definitely The Ghost.

The Ghost’s Conversation with Mischief and Henryk’s Suicide Attempt

Mischief is seen moving through the woods approaching a river and we see Henryk on a bridge looking down. The hand arrives on the riverbank and talks with Mischief.

The Ghost asks Mischief why he is here, and he claims that he wanted to witness a mortal sin and the consequences of bloodguilt adding that it is also suppertime.

Henryk is scene to step out in front of the car filled with the members of his family before the scene cuts to white with signs of Cerebral Electricity. The hand is seen raising Henryk’s grate-granddaughters from the wreck before Henryk is seen unharmed looking into the river.

The Ghost continues talking to Mischief she says unlike him I’m still here. The Ghost then claims that Mischief can change that and requests that Mischief “bring him back” and let Henryk know about his grate-granddaughters survival. Henryk is seen to jump in the river presumably trying to help his family before the scene change back to Mischief and The Ghost, until Mischief can fulfil his end of the deal The Ghost promises to protect her granddaughter.

Mischief asks what he would get in exchange for his help to witch The Ghost offers all that perish.

Ending

We see a finale shot of Henryk crying into his hands at the side of the river after realising what he has done before the scene cuts to black ending the episode.

Analysis

The Mortal Sin and Bloodguilt

The mortal sin is likely referring to Henryk’s planed suicide attempt and the consequence of bloodguilt is likely related to how Henryk feels guilty about having outlived his daughter.

How Henryk Survived

We see Henryk in the road before the crash and then it cuts to white, there is not enough spaces or time available for the car to turn into the river like we see it did. this implies a part of his powers may be protecting him from harm in whatever way is possible.

In this case I believe that Henryk realised a large amount of Cerebral Electricity and blow the car of the road, this is also supported by the fact we see a lot of Cerebral Electricity on the bridge after the crash. In episode 13 we see Henryk perfectly land after a fall which could be a much smaller use of this power.

Mischief And The Ghost’s Deal

The Ghost requests that Mischief brings Henryk back to how he used to be, presumably meaning when he was less depressed. She also requests that Mischief informs Henryk of his grate-granddaughters survival.

In exchange for this Mischief will receive all that perish. This is likely referring to all the Cerebral Electricity In the greetings robotics interfaces.

Notes

This episode finale shows the details of the crash that lead to the death of Henryk’s family.
