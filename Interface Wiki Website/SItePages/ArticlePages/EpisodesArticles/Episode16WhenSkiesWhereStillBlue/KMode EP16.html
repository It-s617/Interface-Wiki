This episode is a direct continuation of episode fifteen with Henryk finally arriving home and remembering an evening spent with his daughter.

Episode 16 When Skies Were Still Blue
Episode Number: 16
Title: When Skies Were Still Blue
Released (DMY): 26/10/19
Video Type: Interface Part 2
Synopsis: Mischief and Henryk begin their search for the long-lost great granddaughter by returning to Henryk's old home in search for clues. Upon entering, the overwhelming memories haunt Henryk, the same thoughts he tried to escape from.

Summary

Opening

The scene opens on a bird eating a frog causing its Cerebral Electricity to be absorbed by Mischief.

Mischief calms that he has just learned a lot about amphibious and that he would like to be one, but it is too dangerous where they are changing into his worm form.

Henryk’s Home Destroyed

Henryk and Mischief finally arrive at his old home and find it in a stat of massive disrepair with the tree outside dead, this is likely the same tree that was focused on in Episode eleven.

Mischief hurry’s inside but Henryk lingers outside for a while focusing on the damage and remembering when he planted the tree with his pregnant wife.

Henryk finally enters his home and finds Mischief siting on the counter in the form of a fat little pink frog, Mischief exclaims “I’m a frog and I’m felling fresh.” Before beginning a monologue talking about the fact that there is nobody there and that there is no life in the house both literally and metaphorically.

Mischief clams they are encased by dead wood and are awaiting a mortician whom they have outlived, this clearly being metaphorical. Noticeable as Mischief discusses this, we see a calendar with the painting Horse and Train on it dated November 2015.

Henryk Memory Of Perogies

The scene changes to a memory of Henryk making perogies with his daughter as she begins to talk about her mother sharing stories about their past.

Henryk’s daughter discuss a story her mum told her from back when skies where still blue about Henryk falling from the skie with no plane in site, during which we see Henryk falling from a blue sky holding large tape reals and sleeping in his parachute.

A shot of a woman walking through the hospital is shown as Henryk’s wife is revealed to be the nurse who looked after him, the daughter clams that the first thing Henryk said upon awaking was that he loved her, despite this being there first-time meeting.

Ending

A finale shot is shown of Henryk standing alone in his house before the episode ends fading to black.

Artworks

Horse and Train

This painting is inspired by a poem by Roy Campbell “I scorn the goose-step of their massed attack And fight with my guitar slung on my back, against a regiment I oppose a brain and a dark horse against an armoured train.”.

The main feature of this painting is the juxtaposition of the horse and the armoured train with the train clearly outmatching the horses in every way, representing the future and the horse representing the past.

The meaning for this episode seems to be contrasting the horses and Henryk. Henryk is the man of the past unable to match the new man, as represented by Mischief who likely represents the train.

Notes

This episode gives some evidence to the idea that Henryk is polish or from a polish family. This is that the perogies mentioned in the episode are a traditional polish food.

The calendar was bean left on the data of November 2015 given the apparent age of Henryk’s great-granddaughter this was likely the date of the crash that killed most of Henryk’s family, leading to Henryk abandoning his home. The show most likely taking places 2-10 years after the crash between 2017-2025.
