Description

This episode focuses on Henryk talking to Mr Greetings in the Interface as he explores his memory of the past and finally reunites with his daughter, leading Mr Greetings to offer Henryk a Faustian Bargain.

Synopsis: Within the simulation generated by the brain of The Mechanical Saccorhytus, Mr. Greetings speaks with Henryk about his past and his role in the future. He gives Henryk a choice, to join him and betray Mischief ... or not.

Summary

Opening

This episode opens on a shot of The Chef who appears dead or to be a statue before we see Henryk waiting for a in an area reminiscent of Piazza D'Italia, a train reminiscent of the one from the painting Horse and Train arrives and Mr Greetings welcomes Henryk shaking his hand.

The Train Ride with Mr Greetings

In the next shot we see shows Mr Greetings on the train talking to Henryk, Mr Greetings warns Henryk that if he faces the back, he will only see his past offering to show him his futter if he turns round but Henryk refuses as he is only interested in his past.

We see a ball covered in cloth through The Window of the train before the cloth is raised reviling a large egg, the train stops, and Henryk is seen approaching a camera to take pictures of the egg with Mr Greetings standing behind him. during this scene Mr Greetings asks Henryk what the cause of his dissociation is and tells Henryk he knows the answers if he looks within.

The eggshell shatters into CE revelling a glowing pink blob inside that turns into a brain and releases an explosion that removes Henryk’s skin leaving only boon as he smokes a cigarette this resembles the painting call Skeleton with a Burning Cigarette, by Vincent van Gogh. During this scene Mr Greetings says to Henryk that all hunted souls remain on earth because they cannot leave, presumably speaking hyperbolically meaning they are too upset or obsessed to move on. He then asks Henryk why he didn’t leave, as we see Henryk’s flesh return.

Mr Greeting the then claims the man of the past is responsible for “complicity in war.” “The advancement of destruction.” And “furthering the divide between the natural and industrialised society.” during this we see a simulation of New York with pipes spewing sewage into a river.

The scene changes to a shot of the streets of New York with the twin towers in the background. Mr Greetings begins to talk about how the man of the past becomes obsolete and clams that Henryk and himself are attempting to revers time but unlike Henryk he’s changed, as he finishes, we see a shot of KAMI inside the twin towers bowing as then a plane collides with the building.

Henryks Daughter in the interfaces

The train is seen to arrive in a flower filed with Henryks Daughter standing in it as Mr Greetings promises that if he makes the right chooses Henryk can be happy again.

Henryk embraces his daughter as she claims that everything is like it was before and that see thought she would never have the chance to see him again.

Mr Greetings gestures to the train and tells Henryk that if he wants to live forever with his memories of the past he can do so in The Interface.

A scene of a dragonfly is shown as Henryk’s daughter asks if Henryk is here to stay.

Mr Greetings tells Henryk that Mischief has choses in his eyes and that he is hiding more than Henryk wishes to know, before claiming that Henryk isn’t free from guilt which lead to him being followed by Mischief. During this we see Henryk’s hat float down being and caught by Henryk.

The train is shown to be returning Henryk to where the episode started, Mr Greetings asks Henryk if he is afraid of Mischief, claiming that if he is not then he has been tricked.

We see Henryk looking at two photos one of his family the other of The Wind.

Ending

Mr Greetings tells Henryk that within The Lighthouse there is a red button and if he presses it, he will have made the right choice.

Analysis

Henryks Possible Death

During one scene we see Henryk getting blasted by a nuke casing him to die before getting covered with Cerebral Electricity this would seemingly imply that Henryk died to a nuclear blast but we also know that Henryk had been already transformed when the sky’s where still blue, meaning before 1943 and the first nuke was detonated in 1945 meaning that unless the events of when the skies where still blue are before Henryk’s transformation this cannot be true.



The Sins of The Man of The Past and There Meaning

Mr Greeting the then claims the man of the past is responsible for several thing which I will list and explain below.


“Complicity in war.”

This likely refers to how Mr Greetings believes that humanity and Cerebral Electricity Monsters are essentially at war which was largely ignored for years.


“The advancement of destruction.”

Is likely referring to the advancement of weapons technology, with stuff like nuclear weapons and KAMI being made.


“Furthering the divide between the natural and industrialised society.”

This is likely referring to the advancement of society after World War Two and how nature was increasingly abandoned.



The Ghosts Freewill

The Ghost is seaming trying to convince Henryk to stay in the interfaces at the cost of Mischief freedom, but it is not clear if she has freewill at the time as she has been absorbed by KAMI.

I do not believe this is even The Ghost in the scene as KAMI is shown to be impersonating her in the final episode, and she has all the information she would need as she should be able to remember everything The Ghost remembers after absorbing her Cerebral Electricity.



The Wind’s Photo

Henryk is seen looking at the photo of his family and the photo given to him by Mischief of The Wind. This was likely to remind Henryk of The Winds warning not to get lost in time.

Artworks

Piazza D'Italia

Piazza D'Italia is a public area in New Orleans design in recognition of the Italian community’s contribution to the city and has become an iconic part of the city since it was opened to the public.

There is likely no particular meaning to this appearing in the show, beyond the fact this is a very iconic and unique looking plaza.

Hores And Train

This painting is inspired by a poem by Roy Campbell “I scorn the goose-step of their massed attack And fight with my guitar slung on my back, against a regiment I oppose a brain and a dark horse against an armoured train.”.

The main feature of this painting is the juxtaposition of the horse and the armoured train with the train clearly outmatching the horses in every way, representing the future.

Well, the original use of the painting was to represent Henryk as the man of the past being passed by modern technology, in this case It is obvious that the horse represents Mischief and progress. The old train representing the past Mr Greetings serves and the horses representing the new world created by Cerebral Electricity.

Skeleton With a Burning Cigarette

This painting was made by Vincent van Gogh while he was at an art school and has little meaning beyond being an interesting painting and a slight satire of his school’s habit of using skeletons as art modals.

I do not believe this has any hidden meanings in the show beyond it is a popular painting that parallels well with Henryk as he and Vincent van Gogh frequently smoked.

Golconda

Golconda was meant to represent how paintings are false reality of their subjects and can be used in a deceptive manner.
In this case it is obviously represented by the verity of floating men but in his other works this appears in many other forms, an example being The Human Condition which shows a painting of a painting in such a perspective that you can barely tell what is what, shown on the right.

It is possible that the false realty of a painting idea represented in the original painting transfers to the series as an allegory for The Interfaces being a false reality but it is more likely just used as a references to The Son Of Man and because Mr Greetings is talking about the multiverse at the time.

Sunrise by the Ocean

This painting was made by Vladimir Kush in 1996 it represents the creation of new life with a verity of different artistic metaphors like the yolk and the sun. This work is similar to Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man by Salvador Dali, Salvador Dali is a common inspiration for Vladimir Kush’s paintings.

In the show the inside of the egg is shown to be a pink blob that then turns into a brain covered in Cerebral Electricity this is likely meant to represent Mischief who was shown as the new man in the painting Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man, in a very similar position.

Essentially, I think this just restates the point of the meaning of Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man in episode 6 that Mischief represents The New Man and progress in the world which Mr Greetings is in stark opposition of.
