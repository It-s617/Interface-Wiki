Analysis

Mischief’s Obligations

Mischief claims that he was dreading his obligations if Henryk had not awaken but does not specify what he means, I will explain now what I think this means.

I think this refers to his deal with The Ghost, Mischief had promised to help Henryk be happy and bring him to his Grand granddaughter. I believe that Mischief would have ever been focused to fulfil an unspecified condition of the deal that would have not been pleasant for him or that he simply would not have received his reward, presumably The Interface.



Mischief ’s Star Monolog, Meaning and References

“When humans first gazed upon the night, did they see stars? A lung, a heart, a hand, an alter in the sky. What is a constellation, but a sum of its parts?” “They didn’t see stars, they saw titans.”

This reflects a common theme of the series that reality is largely based on perception. This line may also provide a parallel to how Cerebral Electricity works.

I believe that there is a double meaning for “Did they see stars? A lung, a heart, a hand, an alter in the sky”

This is a reference in this line which references some of the characters in the series, as shown in episode 18 Lung references to The Wind, Heart references to The Chef, and Hand references to The Ghost.

The stars likely refers to Cerebral Electricity as a whole, this conversation refers to constellations which are largely based on people perception of the sky, similar to Cerebral Electricity Monster who are pure Cerebral Electricity which is being filtered through somebody’s perception of the world, hence their differences in ability and KAMI needing to absorb the Cerebral Electricity of others to gain there powers.

The Alter may refer KAMI as she kills a verity of Cerebral Electricity Monster to gain their powers and alters are commonly associated with the concept of sacrifice.


The telephone starts ringing again as Mischief continues saying “What came first Henryk the baby chicken or the egg? Man, or God? If man came first, they’ll make God. By doing so they become one.”

This still relates to the concept of reality being filtered through perception. Essentially, I believe this question is saying what came first reality or human’s perception of it, though this don’t perfectly map to some of the over lines.


“Every choice we make is a sacrifice. Nothing will stop them. And thus, there never was a choice at all. So, it follows, of your choices is an illusion, so too is your sacrifice.”

This is essentially denying that humans do not have choices or freewill, as we never really have a chose, so we never really decide anything. This is usually based in the hypothesis that a human’s behaver is entirely predictable if you can predict ever verbal involved, whoever this is impossible to test as there is no way to perfectly account for every variable effecting somebody.


“To see through your eyes, the world looks the same to me.” “The mask only fits one way.” “You can look out, but you cannot look in.” “When you gaze up at the night sky, what do you see?”

This is Mischief way of asking KAMI what her perspective of the world is and claiming that she cannot see or understand her own existence.



The Ghost is dead

We see that KAMI is impersonating The Ghost in some of the scenes here as well as taking actions we know that The Ghost Never would like threatening Henryk’s Greet-Granddaughter with a knife. This leads me to believe that when a creature is consumed by Greetings Robotics, they are basically dead and only their forms and memories remain to be used as they please regardless of what the person would want.



Apples

In this episode we see Henryk raiser an apple obscuring his faces before launching at KAMI, and at a different point we see Mischief transform into an apple to be consumed by The Mechanical Saccorhytus.

I believe that in both scenes the apple is meant to represent desires. In the first scene we could see this as Henryk embracing and understanding his desires, him know speaking to be happy and embraces the progressing world.

In the second scene it would represent Mischief desire to infiltrate the Interfaces which is seemingly the motive for doing what he does through the series, especially in regard to his deal with The Ghost. This could also be because of the desire of The Mechanical Saccorhytus as he is shown as incredible glutens and hungry a simple desire but a desire non the less.

This would correlate to the uses of apples at other points in the series as well most noticeable in The Son of Man where a similar metaphor is used. The son of an is also likely a reference in of itself as this painting is frequently referenced throughout the show.

Additional for Mischief the idea of a poisoned apple is common in media going back to story’s like sleeping beauty, there is also the idea of worms infesting apples which is a common theme in media worms are a form that Mischief commonly takes, so this could mean to imitate this symbolism.
