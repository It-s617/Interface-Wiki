Description

This episode focuses on the painting called “Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man” and explores Henryk’s past and powers.

This episode introduces the character of Henryk’s daughter prior to her becoming The Ghost.

Summary

Opening

The episode opens with a shot of Henryk in an elevator going to a higher floor. Mischief then appears in his ball form before explaining that he took the stairs as its good for cardio, Mischief is then interrupted by an announcement in French that he cannot understand.

After the announcement Mischief tries to explain about how regular exercise can change DNA but is again interrupted by an announcement this time in English saying that the museum will close soon. Knowing that the museum is closing Mischief directs Henryk to the painting called Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man.

Mischief’s discussion with Henryk

Mischief explains that the painting competed in 1943 was completed the same year as his transformation into a Cerebral Electricity monster, comparing himself to a chicken breaking through an egg with the egg being a metaphor for the world, the same as in the painting being discussed.

Mischief then compares himself to Henryk and asking him when he had broken free, what he had left behind likely speaking metaphorically. When this is said a picture of a crewmate fused to the hull of a ship is shown likely during the Philadelphia Experiment which implies that Henryk was involved in the event.

Henryk’s convocation with his daughter

The scene then transitions to a memory of Henryk speaking with his daughter interspliced with shots of the painting. Henryks daughter discusses about how when she was younger, she wanted to be a photographer like Henryk so she could “freeze time and bear witness to the world.”
Henryks daughter then continues and says she remembers when her mum died the pastor was staring at Henryk which she did not understand at the time but in hindsight understand that it’s because Henryk was impossible young. This leads to Henryks daughter asking Henryk if she should wait for him when she dies and if Henryk will ever die.

Ending

During the monologue of Henryks daughter Henryk is seen to fail to his neighs before fainting leading to Mischief floating over him frowning likely concerned.

Artworks

Geopoliticus Child Watching the Birth of The New Man

The traditional interpretation of the painting is usually that the Geopolitical child represents the world as it was, and The New Man represents the future and the next generation. This is compered to both Mischief and Henryk.

For Mischief it is clearly implied that he is The New Man the painting is actually altered to have The New Man be pink like Mischief, Mischief also compares himself to the painting and how when he was reborn, he had changed the world. This encourages the metaphor that Mischief and Cerebral Electricity represent progress and the next generation.

For Henryk it is less clear, but my interpretation is that Henryk represents The Geopolitical Child watching the birth of The New Man. Henryks immortality has lead him to watch the world moving on and transforming around him but not being able to fully change to go along with the times, this is also supported by Henryk’s career being a photographer somebody who’s job is to watch the world and record it.

Notes

This episode was the first to introduces Henryk’s daughter prior to her transformation into The Ghost.

This episode was the first to imply that Henryk is a Cerebral Electricity Monster despite appearing human, whether or not his is a Cerebral Electricity monster is never confirmed but it is the only explanation for his supposed immortality and incorrigibility.
